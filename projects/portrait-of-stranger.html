Portrait of a Stranger

Photography & Filmmaking / Digital Media

Objectives

1. Understanding the narrative aspect of art and how photographs can tell a story.

2. Practice principles of composition and depth in portrait photography

Summary

This is a project designed to get students out of the classroom and into the real world where they use their cameras to document stories and narratives of normal people in Taiwan. The goal is to understand how everyone has a story and how this can be expressed through photography. Students need to photograph strangers and interview them to find out their life stories, which will then accompany their portraits and be put on display.

The project follows our unit on understanding aperture, shutter, and ISO and how to manipulate these settings to achieve various effects. This project in particular focuses on understanding aperture and F-stops in achieving depth. Click the images below to student's full project and stories related to each portrait.
